Current Project

What I’m actively building and iterating on right now.

Structured vs Bayesian Neural Networks for Outcome Modeling

Ongoing research project on probabilistic outcome modeling using two architectures: a standard feed-forward neural network and a Bayesian neural network. The pipeline ingests historical data, team-strength style signals, and engineered context features to produce calibrated class probabilities instead of just hard labels.

The Bayesian model exhibits smoother training accuracy and loss curves and yields more stable probability distributions compared with the deterministic network. This makes it better suited for simulations, scenario analysis, and risk-aware decision rules where the full predictive distribution matters.

    Bayesian NN PyTorch Outcome modeling Calibration

Upcoming / Future Directions

Next phases of my outcome-model work and longer-term research ideas.

Context-Aware Outcome Modeling with News, Weather & CTMC Dynamics

Planned extension of my current outcome-model pipeline that incorporates external context (news, reports, and weather) and explicit time evolution. The idea is to use a continuous-time Markov chain (CTMC) style representation to track how match conditions evolve over time while injecting structured signals from text and weather data.

This phase will compare models trained on core structured features vs models augmented with context signals, measuring how much news- and weather-derived features improve calibration, sharpness, and stability of probabilities over multi-step horizons.

    CTMC-style dynamics News & weather features Context modeling

Multi-Agent Simulation with Bayesian Policy Evaluation (future idea)

Longer-term idea to build a simulation framework where multiple agents interact in simple environments (social dilemmas, predator–prey, resource harvesting, trading, etc.). Each agent observes partial state, chooses actions via a policy network, and uses a Bayesian head to model uncertainty over returns.

The goal is to combine a deterministic policy (SNN + policy gradient updates) with a Bayesian neural network head that propagates uncertainty through multi-step rollouts, and then study how uncertainty-aware policies differ from standard RL in terms of stability, cooperation, and behavior in noisy environments.

    Multi-agent RL Bayesian policy evaluation Simulation

LLM Behavior & Safety Modeling (future idea)

Concept for a lightweight “guardrail model” that predicts how a large language model is likely to respond before the full answer is generated. The focus is on learning patterns of prompts, intermediate tokens, and contexts that correlate with safe vs undesired completions.

The long-term aim is to train predictors over prompt–response pairs that can flag risky wording, suggest safer alternatives, and estimate the probability of policy violations, so LLM applications can route, block, or reshape requests in advance rather than reacting afterwards.

    LLM safety Behavior modeling Risk prediction

Other Projects

Additional work in optimization, vision, and forecasting.

Stochastic Routing Optimization for Bread Delivery

Two-stage stochastic TSP with time windows and random arc costs for a delivery network. The model chooses a baseline route and then adapts to disruptions through recourse decisions while penalizing late or missed deliveries.

Compared to a deterministic heuristic baseline, the stochastic model reduces expected delivery cost and cuts late deliveries across sampled scenarios, showing the value of explicitly modeling uncertainty in routing.

    Gurobi Stochastic programming Routing Python
Project Report (PDF)

Facial Expression Detection (ResNet50–ViT Hybrid)

Hybrid ResNet50 + Vision Transformer model for facial expression recognition on low-resolution grayscale images, using transfer learning, augmentation, and cross-validation to improve robustness.

The hybrid architecture improves macro-averaged accuracy over a plain CNN and generalizes better to under-represented emotion classes, showing the benefit of combining convolutional and attention-based features.

    Computer vision ResNet50 ViT PyTorch
GitHub (CV experiments)

News + Stock Forecasting & Trading Agent

Prototype that merges news-derived embeddings with price history to forecast short-term price moves and drive a simple buy/sell agent. Uses mixture-style outputs to capture multi-modal returns and backtests strategies against basic benchmarks.

Adding news-based features improves directional hit-rate and backtest performance compared with a price-only baseline, showing how unstructured information can be turned into useful signals for trading decisions.

    NLP Time series PyTorch Backtesting
